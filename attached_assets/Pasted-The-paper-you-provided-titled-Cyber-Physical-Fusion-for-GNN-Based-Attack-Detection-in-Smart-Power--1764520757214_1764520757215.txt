Single-Modal Dependence: Most studies rely solely on either physical or cyber features, neglecting the important multi-modal CP correlations.

Unrealistic Observability: Current IDSs often assume full system observability, which is not practical in real-world power systems.

Limited Attack Coverage: The focus is primarily on basic attacks like False Data Injection (FDI) and Denial of Service (DoS), overlooking advanced threats such as Ransomware (RW), Reverse Shell (RS), and Backdoor (BD).

Inefficient Models: Many deep learning (DL) models are designed for 2D data, failing to capture the inherent graph-structured nature and spatial correlations of power systems.

Physical Layer: Simulated in real-time using OPAL-RT and the RT-Lab software, based on the IEEE 14-bus and 30-bus test systems.

Cyber Layer: Implemented using Docker containers to simulate components like PLCs, HMIs, routers, and firewalls, configured with a scale-free communication network.

Coupling: The layers are coupled via a TCP connection. Two topologies were created to simulate full and partial observability conditions. Partial observability monitors only the most critical physical nodes (generators and 35% of critical loads).

Attack Scenarios: The generated multi-modal dataset includes benign operations and five types of attacks: Ransomware (RW), Brute Force (BF), False Data Injection (FDI), Reverse Shell (RS), and Backdoor (BD).

GNN-Based IDS The proposed Intrusion Detection System is a supervised GNN model that fuses cyber and physical features.

Graph Modeling: The power system is modeled as a connected, undirected, weighted graph G=(V,E,W), where nodes V are heterogeneous (physical substations and cyber routers), and edges E represent intra- and inter-layer connections.

Core Architecture: It uses a multi-layer architecture with stacked Chebyshev graph convolution layers followed by a dense layer for binary classification (Benign or Malicious). This is chosen to effectively capture spatio-temporal dependencies and topological features.

Data Fusion: The model inputs fused CP features (Table 2) and the adjacency matrix.

Improved Detection Rate (DR): The GNN-based IDSs outperformed benchmarks by up to 26% in DR.

Benefit of CP Fusion: Performing multi-modal CP data fusion improved the DR by up to 16% compared to models using only cyber or only physical features.

Cyber features were more effective for RW, BF, RS, and BD attacks.

Physical features were more effective for FDI attacks.

Robustness to Partial Observability: The DR reduction under partial observability was only 1-2% compared to full observability, confirming the model's robustness in real-world conditions.

Scalability: The GNN-based IDS achieved higher DRs (up to 6% improvement) on the larger IEEE 30-bus system compared to the 14-bus system, suggesting good scalability due to the richer dataset and graph-based architecture
